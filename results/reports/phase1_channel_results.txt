PHASE 1 CHANNEL-LEVEL RESULTS — Topographic Alpha/Gamma Ratio
================================================================
64 channels x 16 subjects per group, MED condition

KEY FINDINGS:
=============

1. HYT HAS THE MOST CHANNELS AT 4/3 — BY FAR
   Fraction of channels with |ratio - 4/3| < 0.10:
     CTR:  0/64  ( 0.0%)
     ISY: 22/64  (34.4%)
     VIP:  0/64  ( 0.0%)
     HYT: 36/64  (56.2%)   <-- over half the scalp is at 4/3

   Fraction within 0.05 of 4/3:
     CTR:  0/64
     ISY:  7/64  (10.9%)
     VIP:  0/64
     HYT: 16/64  (25.0%)   <-- 1 in 4 channels precisely at 4/3

2. THE SHIFT IS GLOBAL, NOT REGIONAL
   HYT ratio < CTR ratio at ALL 64 channels (100%)
   56/64 channels significantly different (p<0.05)
   This is not a localized effect — the entire scalp shifts toward 4/3

3. HYT CHANNELS NEAREST 4/3:
   F8  (frontal):           ratio = 1.3372, |r-4/3| = 0.004
   T8  (temporal):          ratio = 1.3276, |r-4/3| = 0.006
   FC6 (fronto-central):    ratio = 1.3401, |r-4/3| = 0.007
   F7  (frontal):           ratio = 1.3426, |r-4/3| = 0.009
   P9  (parietal):          ratio = 1.3455, |r-4/3| = 0.012
   P10 (parietal):          ratio = 1.3484, |r-4/3| = 0.015

4. REGIONAL RANKING (HYT, proximity to 4/3):
   Temporal (4 ch):           |r-4/3| = 0.041  <-- closest
   Parieto-occipital (9 ch):  |r-4/3| = 0.063
   Fronto-central (9 ch):     |r-4/3| = 0.078
   Frontal (9 ch):            |r-4/3| = 0.083
   Prefrontal (8 ch):         |r-4/3| = 0.091
   Parietal (11 ch):          |r-4/3| = 0.099
   Central (7 ch):            |r-4/3| = 0.115
   Centro-parietal (7 ch):    |r-4/3| = 0.137  <-- farthest

5. ISY (SHOONYA) CHANNELS NEAREST 4/3:
   P9  (parietal):    ratio = 1.3341, |r-4/3| = 0.0007  <-- essentially exact
   P10 (parietal):    ratio = 1.3310, |r-4/3| = 0.0023
   ISY achieves the two single-channel values closest to 4/3 in the
   entire dataset, both at lateral parietal electrodes.

6. VIP IS NOT AT 4/3 AT ANY CHANNEL
   Nearest VIP channel: TP8 at 1.4412 (|r-4/3| = 0.108)
   0/64 channels within 0.10 of 4/3
   Confirms VIP's alpha inflation pushes ratio away from 4/3 everywhere

7. P_MED_2b CONFIRMED IN CHANNEL-LEVEL DATA (DRIVEN vs SELF-SUSTAINING)
   Cross-subject variance per channel:
     CTR: 0.184  (novice baseline)
     ISY: 0.266  (open awareness)
     VIP: 0.287  (focused attention)  <-- highest
     HYT: 0.262  (advanced HYT)

   VIP more variable than ISY: 50/64 channels (78%)
   VIP more variable than HYT: 56/64 channels (88%)
   Paired t-test VIP vs ISY: t=5.756, p<0.0001
   Paired t-test VIP vs HYT: t=6.873, p<0.0001

   This is the Phase 1 confirmation of P_MED_2b:
   VIP (focused attention, 9201h) is MORE variable than ISY (open
   awareness, 2625h) AND HYT (15475h), at 78-88% of channels, with
   p<0.0001. The driven vs self-sustaining distinction is real and
   spatially pervasive.

8. NO ANTERIOR-POSTERIOR GRADIENT
   All groups show <0.01 difference between anterior and posterior ratio.
   The 4/3 ratio is spatially uniform across the scalp.

9. NO HEMISPHERE ASYMMETRY
   All groups show <0.3% left-right asymmetry.
   The effect is bilaterally symmetric.

10. MIDLINE vs LATERAL
    All groups show higher ratio at midline (~0.04 higher).
    Lateral channels are consistently closer to 4/3.

SUMMARY:
  The alpha/gamma ratio = 4/3 is a GLOBAL brain signature in HYT,
  present at >56% of scalp channels, bilaterally symmetric, with
  no anterior-posterior gradient. It is not a regional effect.
  The driven (VIP) vs self-sustaining (ISY, HYT) variance distinction
  is confirmed across 78-88% of channels with p<0.0001.
